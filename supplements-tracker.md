## 🔬 EVIDENCE NOTES

### Strong Evidence ✅
- **Omega-3 (EPA/DHA):** Meta-analyses show improved ADHD symptoms, especially with high EPA ratios. Best for mood + focus combined.
- **Vitamin D3:** Deficiency strongly linked to ADHD severity. Correction improves sleep and mood.
- **Creatine:** Strong evidence for cognitive function, working memory, and fatigue reduction. Safe long-term.
- **Protein:** Not a "supplement" but critical — stabilizes blood sugar → more consistent medication absorption.

### Moderate Evidence 🟡
- **Magnesium Glycinate:** Helps with sleep, anxiety, and some ADHD symptoms. Best form is glycinate (better absorption).
- **Zinc:** Zinc deficiency common in ADHD. Supplementation may improve medication response.
- **L-Theanine:** Good for caffeine-induced anxiety. Can improve focus without jitters.
- **B-Complex:** B vitamins critical for neurotransmitter synthesis. Most beneficial if deficiency exists.

### Mixed/Weak Evidence ⚠️
- **Rhodiola Rosea:** Some studies show benefit for fatigue and stress, others show minimal effect. Worth a 4-week trial if stressed.
- **Ginkgo Biloba:** Limited evidence for ADHD. Not recommended as first-line.
- **Iron supplementation:** Only beneficial if iron-deficient. Test before supplementing (too much is toxic).

## 💡 Rafa's SUPPLEMENT STRATEGY

### Phase 1: Foundation (Weeks 1-2)
**Start with:**
- [ ] ADHD meds only (establish baseline)
- [ ] Omega-3 (brain health)
- [ ] Protein powder (breakfast stability)

**Goal:** Get baseline focus/sleep data before adding variables.

### Phase 2: Deficiency Correction (Weeks 3-4)
**Test bloodwork for:**
- [ ] Vitamin D3
- [ ] Ferritin (iron stores)
- [ ] Zinc
- [ ] Magnesium (RBC magnesium test best)

**Supplement based on results.**

### Phase 3: Fine-Tuning (Weeks 5-6)
**Add targeted supplements:**
- [ ] Magnesium (for sleep)
- [ ] L-Theanine (for anxious focus)
- [ ] Creatine (for cognitive energy)

**One at a time, 2-week trial each.**

### Phase 4: Optimize (Weeks 7+)
Based on your tracking data, keep what works, drop what doesn't.

## 🚨 SAFETY CHECKLIST

**Before starting any new supplement:**
- [ ] Consult your doctor (especially with ADHD meds)
- [ ] Check for interactions (use: Drugs.com or similar)
- [ ] Start low, go slow (½ dose for 3 days first)
- [ ] Track effects for 4-6 weeks
- [ ] Stop if side effects occur

**Warning signs to stop immediately:**
- Severe headaches
- Racing heart / palpitations
- Severe insomnia
- Mood swings or irritability
- Digestive distress (nausea, vomiting)
- Skin rashes or allergic reactions

## 🎯 QUICK REFERENCE

### What to Take When
| Time | Supplements |
|------|-------------|
| **Morning (with breakfast)** | ADHD meds, Omega-3, Vit D3, B-Complex, Zinc, Protein |
| **Mid-morning (as needed)** | L-Theanine (before focus block) |
| **Post-workout** | Creatine |
| **Evening (with dinner)** | Magnesium Glycinate |

### What NOT to Mix
- **Vitamin C + ADHD meds**: Take at least 1 hour apart (vit C can reduce med absorption)
- **Calcium + Iron**: Take at different times (calcium blocks iron absorption)
- **High-dose Zinc + Copper**: Long-term zinc use depletes copper — add copper if zinc >30mg/day for 3+ months

### When to Cycle
- **Rhodiola Rosea**: Cycle 6 weeks on, 2 weeks off (body adapts)
- **Caffeine**: If using L-Theanine, reduce caffeine to avoid tolerance
